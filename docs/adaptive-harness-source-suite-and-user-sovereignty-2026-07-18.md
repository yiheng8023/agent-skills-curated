## Product Value

This repository is not an upstream mirror and does not justify itself by
collecting more public Skills than a user can find. It converts a fast-moving,
uneven capability ecosystem into evidence-backed decisions about safety,
quality, superiority, overlap, redundancy, composition, shortfall coverage,
and lifecycle.

The operating principle is **strict admission, free consumption**. The
repository governs what it approves and what evidence its claims cover. Users
remain free to add, remove, combine, fork, or modify Skills and to choose CC
Switch, direct copying, another manager, or no manager. No mandatory Manager or
Hook is part of the product contract.

User modification creates a user-owned derivative. That freedom is preserved,
but the original digest, update lineage, and curated verification do not
automatically transfer to changed content. This is an evidence boundary, not a
restriction on user control.

## Source Suites And Selective Admission

Unchanged upstream content does not imply whole-suite admission. Review occurs
at four levels: source, suite, component, and capability. For a multi-Skill
source, assess internal dependencies and atomicity before selecting individual
components. A tightly coupled suite is reviewed as a unit; independent Skills
may be admitted selectively without rewriting their bodies.

Every reviewed suite needs a selection manifest that records source identity
and revision, atomicity, included and excluded components, rationale, overlap,
complements, conflicts, preferred routes, Hook presence, update re-review
triggers, and rollback boundaries. Skill admission never implies Hook
admission, and an upstream update never auto-admits new or changed components.

Valid outcomes include whole-suite approval, selected unchanged components,
primary and alternative routes, complementary composition, reference-only,
hold, redundant rejection, and unsafe or incompatible rejection.

## Adaptive Harness

Full-chain coverage does not mean universal structure. It means every phase has
a safe and useful option, including native reasoning, open exploration, no
Skill, no Hook, and no additional structure.

- **Open** preserves native reasoning and divergence for creative, exploratory,
  reversible, and low-risk work.
- **Assist** offers Skills, structure, checkpoints, and verification
  proportionally for complex but reversible work without turning them into
  universal gates.
- **Guarded** requires authority, evidence, verification, and rollback for
  permission, privacy, external-write, destructive, publish, release, or
  similarly high-impact work.

The Harness is negative-boundary-first: it defines what must not be inferred,
authorized, fabricated, or mutated without evidence. Outside hard safety and
authority boundaries, automatic behavior is advisory. New constraints require
an observed or reproducible failure, net-benefit evidence, reversibility, and a
review trigger. Constraints that create more friction than value, or that are
absorbed by improving Agent capabilities, must be relaxed or retired.

## Dynamically Adaptive Self-Authored Capabilities

Any future repository-authored Skill or Hook exists only after a reproducible
residual gap and must be dynamically adaptive rather than a static universal
workflow. Runtime posture may respond to task intent and phase, risk, impact,
reversibility, permission and privacy facts, Agent and host capability profile,
user preference, and observed outcome or friction feedback.

Valid outcomes include native or no-Skill handling, open work, advisory or
structured assistance, guarded execution, human confirmation, and safe fallback.
Hooks retain `off / auto / on`: `auto` is contextual and advisory outside hard
boundaries, while `on` means an explicit selected policy rather than universal
maximum strictness. Decisions must be observable and explainable, and user
override remains available outside the non-waivable safety and authority floor.

Dynamic adaptation must not become hidden self-modification. A Skill or Hook may
select a reviewed runtime path, but it may not silently rewrite its code or
policy, expand permissions, publish changes, or turn feedback into a new trusted
release. Feedback may propose a reviewed next version. Host-specific behavior
requires an explicit capability profile and safe degradation when unsupported.

## Standardization Is Deferred

The current fields, modes, manifests, and review checks are working hypotheses
and observation protocols, not mature hard standards. They may guide discovery,
comparison, trials, and evidence collection, but they must stay correctable as
Agent capabilities and ecosystem practice change.

Standardization becomes eligible only after the chain shows stable repeated
value across multiple independent sources, Agents or hosts, task classes, and
real feedback cycles; its benefit must exceed context, control, and maintenance
cost. A later hard-standard proposal also requires a separate authority and
adoption decision. Until then, disagreement, negative results, simpler native
paths, and retirement evidence are first-class outputs rather than deviations.

## Solution Loop

```text
broad discovery
-> dated shortfall observation
-> reason about the solution shape without forcing a Skill
-> compare native, official, runtime, and already-authorized capabilities
-> compare candidate suites, components, and compositions
-> author minimally only for a reproducible residual gap
-> observe real outcomes and friction
-> retain, relax, replace, or retire
```
