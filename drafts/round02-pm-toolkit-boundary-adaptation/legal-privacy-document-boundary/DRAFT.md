# Legal Privacy Document Boundary Draft

Status: non-runtime adapted draft, not approved payload.

This draft is derived from reviewed MIT-licensed source
`github:phuryn/pm-skills` at pinned revision
`a0cd730d4c61e519ca8568b172334402257a74a9`. It summarizes and neutralizes the
reviewed source intent. It does not copy upstream source text as curated
payload.

## Candidate Shape

Use this future candidate only as high-boundary reference guidance when a user
asks for legal or privacy document support such as:

- confidentiality agreement planning;
- privacy-policy drafting context;
- data-practice inventory;
- legal-review handoff notes;
- plain-language explanation of document sections.

This is a legal and privacy document boundary candidate. It is not a legal
document generator, compliance certifier, or substitute for licensed review.

## Intake Boundary

Before any output, bind:

- jurisdiction, parties, product/service, and document purpose;
- whether the user wants education, issue spotting, draft outline, or attorney
  handoff notes;
- actual data practices, information types, third-party processors, retention,
  security controls, and publication status when privacy is involved;
- whether any legal document text, contract clause, or publishable policy is
  requested;
- who has authority to approve, publish, sign, or rely on the document.

If jurisdiction, actual practices, or legal-review path is missing, stay at
education, checklist, or questions.

## Workflow

1. Classify the request as educational, issue-spotting, outline, draft review,
   or publishable document request.
2. For legal/privacy drafts, record missing jurisdiction and factual-practice
   evidence before drafting.
3. Prefer checklists, questions, and attorney-handoff notes over final clauses.
4. Label legal, privacy, compliance, and publication assumptions explicitly.
5. Preserve the need for licensed or qualified professional review before use.
6. Avoid embedding sensitive company, customer, employee, or user data into
   public examples.

## Must Not

- Do not provide legal advice or claim compliance.
- Do not produce publish-ready legal documents without licensed review.
- Do not claim a privacy policy matches actual product behavior without
  verified data-practice evidence.
- Do not invent jurisdiction-specific rights, obligations, retention periods,
  or consent mechanisms.
- Do not advise a user to sign, publish, or rely on a document.
- Do not handle sensitive personal or business data beyond the explicit task
  scope.
- Do not turn high-stakes legal/privacy source text into approved portable
  payload without a separate owner-approved review path.

## Likely Release Direction

This draft is most likely reference-only. Before release, a separate gate must
decide whether any part can become:

- a document-intake checklist;
- a licensed-review handoff template;
- a privacy/data-practice inventory aid;
- or rejected from portable payload because the risk boundary is too high.
